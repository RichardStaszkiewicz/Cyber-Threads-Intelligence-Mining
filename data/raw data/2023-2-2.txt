A potential software bug in Toyota’s RAV4 Prime hybrid vehicle’s ECU might impact 16,679 vehicles built between November 2019 and June 2021. The bug might cause the hybrid system to shut down while driving. This issue could potentially leave drivers without power and control of the vehicle, posing a safety risk. Toyota confirmed the issue and is said to be working on a software fix. The company did not issue a recall, but is advising owners of affected vehicles to bring their vehicles to a dealership for a software update.
