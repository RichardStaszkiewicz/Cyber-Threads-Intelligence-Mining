According to new data from Tracker, a vehicle tracking and fleet management service, vehicle thefts, among which were keyless vehicles, increased in the first half of 2021 as lockdown restrictions began to be lifted. The volume of theft activity had increased by 32% by the end of June 2021 compared to the start of the year, with Range Rover and Land Rover models as the most targeted vehicles, accounting for 29% of the total stolen vehicles recovered in six months. According to the data, keyless cars continued to be frequently targeted by criminals. Tracker’s latest data reports that 92% of the stolen cars it recovered from January to June were taken by tech-savvy thieves compromising the signal from the vehicle’s key fob.
