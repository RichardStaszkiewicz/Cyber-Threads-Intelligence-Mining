Critical vulnerabilities were found in the Realtek RTL8195A Wi-Fi module, a highly popular standalone, low-power-consumption Wi-Fi hardware module used for embedded devices in several industries such as the agriculture, healthcare, and automotive sectors. The six vulnerabilities, discovered by IoT security company, Vdoo, can be exploited to gain access to the Wi-Fi module and the application processor, allowing an attacker to gain root access and take complete control of a device's wireless communications.
