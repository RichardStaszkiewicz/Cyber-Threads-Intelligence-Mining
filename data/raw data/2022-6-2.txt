Users of Bluetooth devices were negatively impacted by a phenomenon called bluejacking. Among the devices targeted by hackers are Tesla vehicles, which are Bluetooth enabled. Generally, bluejacking involves one Bluetooth device hijacking another one and sending spam advertising or other types of unsolicited messages. Hackers can exploit a Bluetooth technology vulnerabilities to bluejack devices within 10 to 30 feet of the target. Crowded public places are the most common places where bluejacking attacks occur. A major concern about bluejacking is that hackers are able to unlock and lock smart locks and car doors.
