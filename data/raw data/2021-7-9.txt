Thirteen vulnerabilities were found on EVlink charging stations, including EVlink City, EVlink Parking, and EVlink Smart Wallbox. The vulnerabilities lead to various impacts, such as allowing an attacker to perform unintended actions such as gaining unauthorized access to data or gaining unauthorized administrative privileges when accessing the charging station’s web server. It also allows for the hacker to perform unintended actions or access to data when crafted malicious parameters are submitted to the charging station’s web server.
