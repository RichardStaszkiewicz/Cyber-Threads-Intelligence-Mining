BlackBerry, a vehicle software provider, addressed the QNX BadAlloc vulnerability and confirmed that it places many sectors at risk, including automotive systems, heavy machinery, rail, industrial controls, and aerospace. The company found a vulnerability that a remote hacker can exploit in its QNX, microkernel-based, real-time operating system used in many fields, such as automotive systems and commercial vehicles. BMW, Ford, and Volkswagen all use QNX for critical systems in their vehicles, such as driver assistance software.
